What if there were a clearer, more profound way to think about climate change?

Imagine you've filled a pot with water and ice. (Lots of ice.)

You put your hand inside. How cold is it?

You grab your handy thermometer to find out.

nerd tidbit

0° Celsius is the equilibrium at which water and ice coexist.

The temperature will read 0° Celsius

(Or 32° Farenheit if you're an old fart.)

Let's warm up our pot. You place it on the stove and turn on the flame.

What happens?

The temperature begins to rise, right?

Be sure to keep the pot well stirred.

(

)

Wrong.

The thermometer will still read 0° Celsius

What's going on?

The water temperature isn't rising, but the ice is melting.

The ice and water are being heated, but they aren't being warmed.

In fact, the temperature will remain at 0° Celsius until all the ice has melted.

It's not intuitive, but it's true.
